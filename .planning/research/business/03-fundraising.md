## 1. Solo-founder realities (data, not vibes)

**Prevalence is up sharply:**
- Share of new startups with a solo founder rose from **23.7% (2019) to 36.3% (H1 2025)**
  ([Carta Solo Founders Report 2025](https://carta.com/data/solo-founders-report/)).
- ~35% of all new startups in 2024 had a single founder, vs 17% in 2017
  ([WebProNews summary of Carta](https://www.webpronews.com/solo-founders-rise-to-35-in-silicon-valley-but-vc-bias-persists/)).

**But the funding gap is real:**
- Solo-led companies were ~30% of 2024-founded startups but received only **14.7% of cash** raised
  in priced equity rounds that year; 35% of 2024 launches were solo but only **17% of those that
  closed a VC round** by year-end were solo
  ([Carta](https://carta.com/data/solo-founders-report/), [Slashdot summary](https://slashdot.org/story/25/08/28/1540255/solo-founders-are-battling-silicon-valleys-biggest-bias)).
- H1 2025 VC-backed company mix: 37% two founders, 25% three, **20% one**, per Startup Valuation
  Delta ([Equidam](https://www.equidam.com/the-case-for-solo-founders/)).

**Counter-evidence to keep in the back pocket:**
- One frequently-cited analysis found **52.3% of successfully exited startups had a single
  founder** ([solofounders.com](https://solofounders.com/blog/fundraising-as-a-solo-founder-the-data-proves-you-wont-pay-a-solo-tax) —
  **verify the underlying study before quoting to a VC**; it's an advocacy source).
- The 2025-26 zeitgeist has shifted: "one founder + AI agents" is now an explicitly underwritten
  thesis at many pre-seed funds ([Entrepreneur Loop playbook](https://entrepreneurloop.substack.com/p/building-a-startup-alone-solo-founder-playbook) — directional).

**What this means for Pedro specifically:**
1. The solo penalty is priced mostly at the *screening* stage — warm intros (which he has) skip
   exactly that stage.
2. The standard objections are "no one to argue with," "key-person risk," "can't recruit." The
   pre-emptive answers: (a) demonstrated shipping velocity with committed quality gates
   (screenshot harness, adversarial tenancy suites — show the repo); (b) an advisor bench drawn
   from his VC/PE contacts; (c) an explicit first-hires plan (see `07-solo-vs-team.md`).
3. **ASSUMPTION:** Pedro's ex-VC-sole-engineer profile reads as "technical founder who already
   understands how funds think" — genuinely differentiating at pre-seed, where the team IS the
   diligence.

## 2. 2025-26 pre-seed/seed norms (instruments, sizes, caps)

**Instrument:** the **post-money SAFE with a valuation cap, no discount** is the standard
pre-seed instrument; the threshold where rounds switch to priced equity has risen to roughly
**$4M+** ([Carta State of Pre-Seed 2025](https://carta.com/data/state-of-pre-seed-2025/),
[Q2 2025 edition](https://carta.com/data/state-of-pre-seed-q2-2025/)).

**Median post-money SAFE valuation caps (Carta, 2025):**

| Round size | Median cap |
|---|---|
| < $250K | **$7.5M** (Q2 2025, up from $6.5M) |
| $250K – $1M | **~$10M** |
| $1M – $2.5M | **~$15M** |

([Carta State of Pre-Seed 2025](https://carta.com/data/state-of-pre-seed-2025/),
[Carta Q2 2025](https://carta.com/data/state-of-pre-seed-q2-2025/))

**Seed (priced) norms:**
- Median US seed pre-money hit **~$16M** (Q3 2025), up from $14.8M FY2024; median seed round
  **~$3.1–4M**; typical dilution **~20%**
  ([Carta via Causo Hub](https://hub.causo.ai/guides/seed-valuation-2026),
  [ValueAddVC medians](https://valueaddvc.com/blog/startup-funding-rounds-in-2025-whats-normal-at-pre-seed-seed-a-and-b)).
- AI-attached companies command a **1.5–2× valuation premium** at seed
  ([IdeaProof benchmarks](https://ideaproof.io/fundraising-benchmarks),
  [Flowjam on Carta data](https://www.flowjam.com/blog/seed-round-valuation-2025-complete-founders-guide)).
- Deals under $500K made up nearly half of all pre-seed rounds in 2025; the common pattern is a
  small angel SAFE ($100–500K) to hit a milestone, then a larger pre-seed at a higher cap
  ([Kruze Pre-Seed Guide 2025-26](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/preseed-funding/)).
- Angel checks: **$25–150K**; micro-VC first checks: **$100–500K**
  ([Angel Investors Network guide](https://angelinvestorsnetwork.com/venture-capital/pre-seed-funding-guide)).
- YC standard deal (benchmark even if not applying): **$500K = $125K for 7% + $375K uncapped
  MFN SAFE** ([YC](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/ycs-500-000-standard-deal)).

**The bar has moved between stages:**
- Median seed→Series A gap reached **~616 days** (mid-2025) — plan runway accordingly
  ([Startups.com seed lexicon](https://www.startups.com/lexicon/seed-round)).
- Median Series A company had **~$1.5M ARR** in 2025 (Carta, via
  [CRV KPI guide](https://www.crv.com/content/key-performance-indicators)); seed leads
  increasingly want **$50–200K ARR or an equivalently strong traction signal**, and burn
  multiple <2.0x is watched ([Forum VC benchmarks](https://www.forumvc.com/thought-pieces/revenue-reality-check-funding-benchmarks),
  [Culta milestones](https://culta.ai/blog/startup-financial-milestones-by-stage)).

**ASSUMPTION / caution:** blog aggregations of Carta data drift; before any live conversation,
pull the current quarter's Carta State of Pre-Seed PDF and quote it directly.

## 3. Staged expectations mapped to polytoken

| Stage | Typical package (2025-26) | What investors need to see | polytoken status (2026-07-22) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Friends/angels** | $100–500K on SAFE, ~$7.5–10M cap | Working demo, credible founder, a wedge | Demo exists; **live-use gap (LIVE-03/04) must close first** |
| **Pre-seed (institutional)** | $500K–$1.5M on SAFE, ~$10–15M cap | Live product, early usage cohort, retention signal, clear wedge narrative | Not yet: needs real users beyond founder |
| **Seed** | $2.5–4M priced/SAFE, ~$16M pre (AI premium possible) | $50–200K ARR *or* exceptional engagement/growth; burn multiple <2x | 12–24 months away, gated on monetization |
| **Series A** | — | ~$1.5M ARR median, retention + efficiency | Horizon planning only |

**The honest sequencing conclusion:** polytoken today is *pre*-fundable on traction but
*unusually strong* on artifact (a working, architecturally novel product built solo). The
highest-ROI moves before any raise, in order:
1. Close the live loop (own real email flowing, CLUS-07 scenario proven) — zero dev cost.
2. Get 10–50 real outside users through the forwarding flow; instrument retention (feeds
   track 08).
3. Only then choose: angel SAFE now at a modest cap vs. waiting for pre-seed leverage.

**Dilution math to keep in mind (illustrative, ASSUMPTION-free arithmetic):** $500K on a $7.5M
post-money cap = 6.7%; $1M on $10M = 10%; a later $3M seed at $16M pre ≈ 15.8% more. A solo
founder stacking angel + pre-seed + seed on these medians still holds ~65–70% before Series A —
solo founding materially softens lifetime dilution pressure.

## 4. Leveraging the existing VC/PE network (the actual plan)

Pedro's edge: he was a VC firm's sole engineer and has experienced VC/PE contacts. Data point on
why this matters: most pre-seed checks are relationship-originated, and warm-intro deals clear
screening at far higher rates than cold inbound (directionally supported across
[Kruze](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/preseed-funding/) and
[Angel Investors Network](https://angelinvestorsnetwork.com/venture-capital/pre-seed-funding-guide);
**ASSUMPTION:** no reliable public conversion-rate stat — do not quote a number).

Recommended sequence:
1. **Advice-first, capital-second.** Run 3–5 conversations with the VC/PE contacts explicitly
   framed as "pressure-test my memo" (this folder is that memo). Investors who shape the plan
   convert to first checks and to intros at much higher rates. Never ask the same person for
   advice and money in the same meeting.
2. **Recruit 1–2 as angels/advisors** (standard advisor grants: 0.1–0.5% for pre-seed advisors,
   vesting; **ASSUMPTION:** norms vary — confirm against current FAST-agreement templates).
   A respected VC/PE name on the cap table de-risks the solo-founder screen for everyone after.
3. **The ex-employer question:** if the firm Pedro worked for invests at this stage, they are
   either the natural first check or a conflict to be disclosed early. Decide deliberately;
   an ex-employer passing quietly reads badly, so secure either a check or an explicit,
   repeatable "we don't do pre-seed" narrative.
4. **Use the network for diligence prep, not just intros:** ask contacts what *they* would need
   to see at seed for this product (feeds track 08's milestone definitions), and which funds
   have an active "AI-leveraged solo founder" or "personal AI" thesis.
5. **Kaszek-adjacent note — ASSUMPTION:** the repo references `kaszek-os-dev` as a prior-art
   reference, suggesting the VC relationship may be with a top-tier LatAm fund; if so, that
   network reaches both US and Brazilian early-stage capital. Handle any IP-adjacency questions
   cleanly in `02-incorporation-and-legal.md` before pitching that network.

**Angel vs institutional at this stage:** prefer angels/operators from the network for the first
$100–300K (fast, light governance, no signaling risk), and delay institutional pre-seed until a
usage story exists — a priced-lead pass at pre-seed creates negative signal; an angel SAFE does
not ([Kruze](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/preseed-funding/)).

## 5. Brazil / cross-border structuring (fundraising-relevant summary; details in track 02)

- US institutional investors overwhelmingly prefer wiring into a **Delaware C-corp on standard
  SAFE docs**; matching that template speeds diligence and widens the capital pool
  ([Sacra chat on LatAm Delaware preference](https://sacra.com/chat/h/46f39e0d-c727-4ca0-bae3-2de50d115839/),
  [Latitud incorporation guide](https://latitud.com/blog/startup-incorporation-guide-corporate-structure)).
- Brazilian founders commonly use the **"Delaware Tostada"** (Delaware entity atop the Brazilian
  operating company) when raising angel/pre-seed/seed; funds publicly comfortable with it include
  500 Startups, Canary, Maya Capital, Norte Capital, ONEVC, and Latitud Fund
  ([Latitud on the Tostada](https://latitud.com/blog/delaware-llc-whats-a-tostada-and-why-your-startup-might-need-it)).
- **Latitud Ventures** leads LatAm pre-seed with ~**$250K checks** — a natural fit given profile
  and geography ([Latitud Ventures announcement](https://latitud.com/blog/latitud-ventures-announces-new-venture-capital-fund-to-lead-pre-seed-in-latam)).
- **ASSUMPTION:** if the near-term user base and network are US/global, incorporate Delaware
  C-corp directly rather than Tostada-converting later; decide in track 02 with counsel before
  the first SAFE is signed (repapering after checks is expensive).
